Hello, I am Nico!

Welcome to my website. The content here is centered around programming, statistics, psychology, philosophy, and history.

My technical interest spans big data systems, distributed computing, and statistical analysis. I work primarily in Python, Rust, Go, TypeScript, and SQL. I'm particularly drawn to solving the hard systems questions that make large-scale data science possible—and in the hidden layers that shape UX from behind the scenes.

In my free time, I enjoy reading fantasy novels and historical texts, drawing and cartography, playing D&D 5e and strategy games (Total War, ArmA, isometric RPGs), and long-distance running around Madison.

Writing

I try to stay away from being prescriptive and instead prefer to frame my experiences and how I've learned or grown from them. I want to stay away from making sweeping or unsubstantiated claims about complex topics. I focus on my interests in programming, statistics, psychology, philosophy, and history, and that's what I write about.

I am forever learning, and formulating my interests and perspective in essays allows me to refine my beliefs and understanding. I aim to put out a piece once per month. I am always open to critiques, fact checking, or revising to cover angles I did not anticipate.

Colophon

A colophon is a brief statement that appears at the end of a book, providing information about its production. I got the idea from Andrew, and while I don't believe I will do as great a job as he did with his, here is my best shot:

There's a running joke among my friends that I go through many websites. This one will hopefully stand the test of time. I've had a lot of fun writing this using only HTML/CSS with a build.sh script, Pandoc, and post templates for the blog. I have enjoyed that I have to figure things out rather than getting features out of the box, as it forces me to really consider what I actually want.

The site is intentionally minimal, with no JavaScript except for conditional loading of MathJax on blog posts that require LaTeX rendering. Content is processed from Markdown files using Pandoc, creating a static site that's lightweight and fast. I subset all fonts using pyftsubset to include only the characters actually used on each page. The simplicity of the system makes maintenance straightforward.
